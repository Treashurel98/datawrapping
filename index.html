Illegal Dumping Continuosly Increases Following Pandemic Cuts in Jamaica, Queens

By: Treashure Lewis

November 11, 2021

Illegal Dumping has casually been an issue within Jamaica, Queens, but as of recently, it has become a central issue that citizens say needs to be addressed. The Department of Sanitation is said to be responsible for cleaning the streets and eliminating trash. There are designated pickup locations and dumping locations for trash that DOS employees acquire, however, with the decrease in workers, the amount of trash for the remaining workers to pick up seems to be unmanageable. This has led to the stark increase in illegal dumping within Jamaica, Queens. It is unclear why Jamaica's illegal dumping cases is significantly higher than the cases in other Queens, NY neighborhoods, but Community Board 12 members are currently working to find both a solution and an answer to this problem. Aracelia Cook, a Community Board 12 member, and a Jamaica, Queens resident states that "this issue has gone on way too long; we need to work hard to bring awareness to this issue as it is now an indicator of poor living conditions within our streets."
